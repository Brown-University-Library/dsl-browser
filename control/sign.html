Our Tempestuous Sun

When our nearest star is stormy, it results in streams of energized particles that can create auroras here in Earth, and can even disrupt cell phones and power grids.

Observing our Sun in ultraviolet light, we see its turbulent atmosphere, where the loops are constantly changing areas of energy and magnetism that drive what is known as "space weather." The massive ejection shown on the lower left erupted on August 31, 2012.
